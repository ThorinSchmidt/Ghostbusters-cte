My Ghostbuster lost Trait Points. What now?

Losing Trait points is a fairly big deal, although it is better than dying. The first thing though, is to look at why you lost Trait points. Any points lost because you exchanged them for Brownie points are gone, gone, gone. There is a way to get those back, but involves spending Brownie Points to do it, which is detailed in the Brownie points section. This section deals with temporary Trait point losses due to damage your Ghostbuster sustained during an adventure

Regular Bed Rest

This is the simplest, least expensive method. Your Ghostbuster goes home, goes to bed (or couch, we don't judge) and pretty much stays there. This is a great option if you have caring friends who will bring you food, you have a mom/grandma with a large supply of chicken soup, or there is a handy pizza place that delivers.

For each day you spend resting at home, doing nothing else(except watching TV or light reading), roll a Ghost Die. If the result is a Ghost, your day of rest was unsucessful (your friends come over and convince you to Monopoly, your Aunt/Uncle comes over to "help you recuperate", etc.). Otherwise, on a result of 1-5, congratulations! You just regained 1 Trait point! What? Yes, only 1. I said this was the simplest and cheapest option, not the most effective.

ER/Urgent Care Visit

This is an option if the damage is bad, but not too bad. It can be dicey, however. First roll a Ghost Die, if you roll a Ghost, the facility is swamped, and they just can't handle another patient. Either go home, or go to the Hospital. On a roll of 1-5, they can see you! In that many hours.

Once you are in with the doctor/nurse, roll another Ghost Die(told ya this was dicey!). If a Ghost is rolled, you are too complicated, and they send you to the Hospital(Bed Rest at home is no longer a viable option!). On a roll of 1-5, you gain back that many Trait Points(up to your max)! Huzzah! If that does not heal you fully, you can then go home and recover the rest in bed, or go to the hospital for more help. You cannot choose this option until after you have managed to regain all your Trait points at least once.

This option is more expensive than resting at home, but cheaper than the Hospital. The actual costs are left to your GM, but a recommended ballpark cost is $2,500 per Trait point recovered. Plus any ambulance fees, if any. Note that if you are transferred to the hospital, there will be ambulance fees. Wait, what if you went to an ER? Sorry, the only available bed is at another hospital.

Hospital Stay

A hospital stay is the most effective and most expensive recovery method. Any Non-USA readers, please note, I'm exaggerating things a bit for comedy, but not by much!

A visit to the hospital always takes a minimum of three days. Roll 3d6 (1 Ghost Die, 2 regular dice). You recover that many Trait points up to your max. Keep track of the total amount rolled. If you didn't recover all your points, then you must stay another day until fully healed. The hospital will not dischargr you until then. For each additional day, roll the Ghost Die, and add that amount to the previous total. If at any time a Ghost is rolled, you only recieve half the total amount rolled. Round fractions up, minimum of 1.

The cost for your stay is a little complex. The actual cost per point is ultimately up to the Ghostmaster, but it is normally twice the cost of the ER rate. In this case, $5,000 per point rolled. Yes, the amount you rolled, not the amount you healed. In addition, if at any time a Ghost was rolled, multiply the final cost(including ambulance and ER fees, if any) by 1.5.

For instance, Beverly lost a total of 6 trait points dealing with a Carnivorous Ice Cream Truck. She goes to the ER, and is seen. BBeverly's player then rolls a Ghost, and is sent to the hospital (minimum ER fee plus ambulance ride) Once in the hospital, her player rolls a 4, 4, and a Ghost. Normally, Beverly would recover 8 Trait points, but the Ghost makes it 4. Beverly stays another day. For next day, Beverly's player rolls a 5! yay! She is discharged from the hospital and receives a bill for $102,750. And no, I am not exagerrating all that much!

It breaks down like this:

  • $ 2,500 - Minimum ER fee
  • $ 1,000 - Ambulance fee
  • $65,000 - hospital stay 13 points rolled x 5000 each.
    --------------------------------------------------------------
  • $68,500 - Normal cost
  • 1.5 multiplier (rolled a Ghost. 2, actually.)
  • $102,750 - Final cost due to "Complications"

The actual nature of the complications is left to the fertile mind of the Ghostmaster. Msybe surgery was required, or extra tests, maybe a ghost invaded the hospital (in which case some horse-trading ahould reduce the bill, assuming the Ghostbusters handle it with little collateral damage).

Final Thoughts

There you have it. Three fun ways to enable your Ghostbuster to regain lost Trait points! Enjoy!
